Dad, this is what I go through.

This is ADHD Paralysis.

What It Feels Like

It's a feeling of being completely stuck or frozen, even when I desperately want to get going.

"I want to do something. I even take a step to do it. Then I look up and time has passed, but I still haven't done the thing."

The Why

It's not a choice or a sign of laziness. It's about how my brain works. When it gets stressed or overwhelmed, it can just... freeze. The wiring for executive functions—like planning and starting—gets jammed.

Task Overwhelm

The task feels like a giant, tangled knot. I don't know where to even start.

Loud Emotions

Fear of failing or doing it wrong can be so loud it's paralyzing.

The Avoidance Loop

I avoid it, feel bad, the task gets scarier, so I avoid it more.

How I Try to Break Free

Chop It Up

Break a big task into tiny, silly-small steps.

The 5-Minute Rule

Just do it for 5 minutes. Sometimes that's enough to start.

Be Kind to Myself

This is hard. I try not to beat myself up over it.
